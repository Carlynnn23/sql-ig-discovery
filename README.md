Project Overview
This project aims to analyze the performance of 20 Instagram accounts by examining their post types (images, reels, carousels) over a one-year period (November 2024 – November 2025). The data has been meticulously cleaned and analyzed using PostgreSQL, which involved removing null values and blank spaces. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was conducted, focusing on date and engagement metrics, resulting in the generation of new metrics such as post frequency and average weighted interactions for a Power BI data visualization report.

Key Insights from the Visualization Report:

1. Optimal Posting Times: Maximizing Engagement (Heatmap)
   Analysis of engagement rates reveals that the ideal time to post is Tuesday at 2 AM (UTC), while the least effective times are Monday at 3 PM and 6 PM (UTC).
   
2. Content Format Success: What Works Best?
   The most effective combinations of content types and formats are as follows: Carousels perform best with fashion content, images excel in the fitness category, and reels are most successful with photography.

3. Future Trends in Content Exposure: A Look Ahead
   Carousels have shown a steady increase in visibility throughout the year, while reels appear to be declining in discoverability.

4. Posting Frequency Insights: Timing for Viral Success
   Posts made within the first 12 hours of creation exhibit the highest potential for "viral" performance. Conversely, posting between 1-2 days significantly correlates with "low" performance content. Therefore, the optimal posting interval is within the first 12 hours after content creation.

5. Follower Conversion Success: Identifying Top Performers
   Utilizing the slicer tool to sort data by account ID reveals which accounts have achieved the highest follower conversion rates from initial impressions, highlighting the key accounts to be studied upon.
